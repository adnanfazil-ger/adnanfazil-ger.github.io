About Me

A brief introduction to who I am and what drives my research.

I am an Applied Mathematician & Mathematical Engineer with a deep passion for geometric analysis, the calculus of variations, and partial differential equations. I recently completed the prestigious International Double Master's program (RealMaths Consortium), obtaining an MSc in Mathematical Engineering from the University of L'Aquila (Italy) and an MSc in Applied and Interdisciplinary Mathematics from Brno University of Technology (Czech Republic).

My research focuses on geometric curvature energies, regularity theory for variational problems, and the structure of geodesics in sub-Riemannian spaces. For my Master's thesis, I analyzed geodesics in sub-Riemannian geometry, deriving Euler–Lagrange equations and investigating normal vs. abnormal extremals under the supervision of Prof. Aleš Navrát.

I thrive on translating complex, abstract mathematical formulations—such as constrained variational principles and differential geometry—into concrete computational studies, employing tools like Python, MATLAB, and finite element solvers like COMSOL Multiphysics.
